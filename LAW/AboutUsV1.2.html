The Live Action Spirit

The Live Action Spirit is derived from our favorite television star and fellow Appalachian, Ernie Lee Brown, Jr., better-known as "The Turtle Man". The Turtle Man embodies the blue-collar, light-hearted spirit that has made LAW into what it is today. Live Action Woodworking (LAW) has been an official organization since 2014. Unofficially, though, we have over 100 years of combined woodworking experience and have been working together since bell-bottoms and discos were in style. When we moved into our shop in downtown Roanoke, "Not Responsible" was the phrase painted on the wall. We took it as a sign and liked it so much, we made it our official slogan.

How We Operate

We like to keep things local, whether it's our equipment, projects, lumber, or employees. Live Action only uses locally-harvested wood (primarily walnut) from a sawmill in Southwest Virginia. After building projects for companies such as Black Dog Salvage, we decided to create our own company. Today, we primarily serve local companies such as Oakey's funeral home and local churches in addition to creating custom furniture pieces for individuals in the area. We take pride in the fact that we hire hard-working individuals who may otherwise face barriers to employment. We believe that putting people to work, and more importantly- putting people to work in a positive, supportive work environment can and does create a positive change at both the individual and societal-level.

Mike Beamer

Mike is no stranger to the Blue Ridge, as he was born and raised in Salem, Virginia. He taught woodshop in the Roanoke County school district for 32 years after graduating from Virginia Tech in Blacksburg, where he would meet his wife, Dianna. When he's not running the saw, Mike loves to run and train for 5k's and half-marathons. Mike serves his community through a variety of different ways. He currently volunteers at the jail Bible ministry through a local organization in addition to participating and assisting in the facilitation of First Christian Church's "Men U" Group. His favorite place in the world: Sedona, Arizona.

Jeff Beamer

Like his brother, Jeff was also born in Salem. Jeff attended Radford University, where he studied finance. He has been a financial advisor for over 20 years, which has allowed him to be a vital piece of his and Mike's real estate business. Jeff is the founder and leader of the "Men U" group at First Christian Church, which is in its seventh year! Jeff also plays a huge role in working with the Youth Group at First Christian, where he has helped mentor and facilitate the personal and spiritual development of many teenagers and young adults.

David Topping

David met the Beamer brothers in the early 80's and has enjoyed woodworking with them ever since. His motto is to always laugh hard and to work some, too..most of the time. With over forty years of woodworking experience, he is confident that LAW can accomplish anything that it sets its mind to. David is a cruicial piece of Live Action Woodworking and truly personifies the "Live Action" mindset by working hard and having some fun along the way.
